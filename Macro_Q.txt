Here’s the complete list of macro-financial indicators with their stock market linkage and key correlations to other variables:

⸻

📊 Macroeconomic Indicators
	•	GDP Growth
	•	📈 Linkage: Positive correlation; higher GDP → increased corporate earnings → bullish equities
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Unemployment (inverse), Consumer Confidence, Industrial Production
	•	Unemployment Rate
	•	📉 Linkage: Negative correlation; rising unemployment often signals recessions and falling markets
	•	🔗 Correlates with: GDP (inverse), Inflation, Consumer Confidence
	•	Consumer Confidence Index (CCI)
	•	📈 Linkage: High confidence supports consumption and stock performance
	•	🔗 Correlates with: GDP, Unemployment, Retail Sales
	•	Inflation Rate (CPI)
	•	🧨 Linkage: Moderate inflation supports equities; high inflation is a headwind
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Interest Rates, Money Supply, Commodity Prices
	•	Industrial Production Index
	•	📈 Linkage: Positive correlation with market strength; reflects economic output
	•	🔗 Correlates with: GDP, Employment, Energy Demand
	•	Money Supply (M2)
	•	💧 Linkage: More liquidity boosts short-term equity rallies; too much → inflation
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Inflation, Interest Rates, Credit Growth

⸻

💸 Monetary & Credit Indicators
	•	Short-Term Interest Rates (Fed Funds Rate)
	•	📉 Linkage: Rising rates reduce equity valuations via discounting
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Inflation, Yield Curve, Money Supply
	•	Long-Term Interest Rates (10-Year Yield)
	•	🤔 Linkage: Mixed; may reflect growth or inflation fears
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Inflation Expectations, CAPE, Mortgage Rates
	•	Yield Curve Slope (10Y-2Y)
	•	⏳ Linkage: Inversion signals recession risk; flattening curve is bearish
	•	🔗 Correlates with: GDP, Equity Returns, Credit Growth
	•	Corporate Credit Spreads
	•	📉 Linkage: Widening spreads signal credit stress and often precede equity downturns
	•	🔗 Correlates with: VIX, Financial Stress Index, Risk Sentiment
	•	Financial Stress Index (STLFSI, NFCI)
	•	🛑 Linkage: High stress negatively impacts stock markets
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Credit Spreads, VIX, Consumer Confidence

⸻

📈 Valuation and Equity Market Indicators
	•	Valuation Ratios (CAPE, P/E)
	•	🔁 Linkage: High valuations predict lower long-run returns
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Earnings Yield, Dividend Yield, Inflation Expectations
	•	Market Capitalization to GDP
	•	🧭 Linkage: Over 100% suggests overvaluation; lower = better forward returns
	•	🔗 Correlates with: GDP, Corporate Profits, Risk Appetite
	•	Equity Risk Premium
	•	💹 Linkage: Low ERP = expensive market; High ERP = attractive returns
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Treasury Yields, Inflation, Corporate Profits

⸻

📉 Volatility & Sentiment Indicators
	•	VIX (Volatility Index)
	•	🚨 Linkage: Spikes in VIX signal risk-off; often coincides with or precedes sell-offs
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Credit Spreads, Stress Indices, Market Reversals
	•	Investor Sentiment (Bull/Bear Surveys)
	•	📊 Linkage: Extreme sentiment often contrarian indicator (e.g., high bullishness = top)
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Equity Flows, Volume, Valuations
	•	Consumer Sentiment (UMich, Conference Board)
	•	😃 Linkage: Rising sentiment supports discretionary sectors and risk assets
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Retail Sales, Stock Returns, Employment
	•	Economic Policy Uncertainty Index
	•	🌫️ Linkage: High uncertainty suppresses equity valuations, especially cyclical sectors
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Volatility, Capital Spending, FX Risk
	•	Margin Debt (NYSE)
	•	🧨 Linkage: High leverage often precedes market peaks and corrections
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Equity Returns, Interest Rates, VIX
	•	Trading Volume / Turnover
	•	🌀 Linkage: High turnover = speculative behavior; changes may precede market reversals
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Sentiment, Margin, Liquidity

⸻

🛢️ Commodities & Global Indicators
	•	Oil Prices (WTI, Brent)
	•	🔥 Linkage: Rising oil = inflation, input costs; can hurt broad equity indexes
	•	🔗 Correlates with: CPI, Industrial Production, USD Index
	•	Gold Prices
	•	🛡️ Linkage: Safe-haven; spikes during stress or inflation fears
	•	🔗 Correlates with: VIX, CPI, Real Rates
	•	Commodity Price Index (CRB, GSCI)
	•	📦 Linkage: Rising prices can signal strong global demand or rising inflation
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Industrial Production, CPI, Oil Prices
	•	Baltic Dry Index (BDI)
	•	🚢 Linkage: Leads trade cycles; rising BDI = bullish for industrials/materials
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Global PMI, Oil Prices, GDP
	•	US Dollar Index (DXY)
	•	💵 Linkage: Strong dollar can suppress earnings; inversely linked to commodities
	•	🔗 Correlates with: Oil, Gold, Exports
	•	Global Stock Indices (FTSE, Nikkei, MSCI EM)
	•	🌍 Linkage: International market trends often spill into U.S. equities
	•	🔗 Correlates with: FX Rates, Trade Balance, Risk Appetite
